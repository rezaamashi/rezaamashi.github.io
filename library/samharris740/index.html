Free Will

Reconciling with the Deterministic Reality of Being Human

After reading this book, I get that Harris’ approach on reconciling the fact of the inexistence of free will could lead us into a different path that was previously thought to be the default of determinism for most of the free will proponents, which is fatalism. Harris’ position was that the realization of the inexistence of free will could enhance the sympathy of people towards each other.

Einstein quote on Schopenhauer reflect a great explanation on Harris’s position in this short book/essay

A man can will what he do, but he can’t will what he will.

Argument for determinism

On building his argument for determinism, in contrast of metaphysical approach of libertarianism, Harris stand upont many scientific researchs which found that each of one’s conscious decision came about 300 miliseconds before one realize of making such decision. In a way of which one’s decision come about from deeper unconscious reason, such as hunger or lethargy. Such inability to find the underlying reason of why one’s decision came about is regarded as the argument for the nonexistence of free will. Although, it’s explicable that a reason of one’s thought or will came from the situation and life experience one has been exposed with this would only strengthen the argument for the inexistence of free will or the illusory state of it.

On morality and justice system

A deeper understanding seeing human and people as part of the nature is that there could be a more sympathetic and pragmatic approach towards punishment, whether with the goal of retribution or deterrent. A judge could approach punishment of a suspect through his biological and experiential disposition. Instead of approaching it through the free will lense, of which each actions are part of the suspect understanding and psyche. Judge could asses how the fact of the suspect’s upbringing or certain biological conditionmdashsuch as inclination of doing the crime which is caused by certain shape of tumor in his brain.

On luck and success

Harris’s said that even the ability to resist laziness is something that also came about from one’s personal experience and manifest as a skill that could be harnessed throughout one’s life. Notwithstanding the actual situation of genetic predisposition, political climate of the location where one’s born and developing in, the level of emotional maturity of one’s parent, the financial status of one’s parent, etc. Those are affecting one’s position and one’s ability to reach success.
