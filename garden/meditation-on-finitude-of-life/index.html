My journey on reconsiliation with the finitude reality of life and living

I was reading Oliver Burkeman’s works in the past few days. Notably Four Thousand Weeks (Burkeman 2021) and The Antidote (Burkeman 2012). And currently reading Ernest Becker’s The Denial of Death The books explore on how human are faced with dichotomy of being a symbolic and physical being.

From Four Thousand Weeks I got the inspiration to add a weekly counter from the day I born to now into my journal in org mode. Here is the snippet and how I applied it using org-journal:

(defun rz/calculate-week-counter (birth-year birth-month birth-day)
  "Calculate the number of weeks from the given birthdate to the current date."
  (let* ((birth-date (encode-time 0 0 0 birth-day birth-month birth-year))
         (current-date (current-time))
         (seconds-per-week (* 60 60 24 7))
         (weeks (floor (/ (- (float-time current-date) (float-time birth-date)) seconds-per-week)))
         (month-name (format-time-string "%B" (encode-time 0 0 0 birth-day birth-month birth-year))))
      (message "%d weeks since %s, %d %d" weeks month-name birth-day birth-year)))

;; Example: If you were born on January 4th, 1999
;; (rz/calculate-week-counter 1999 1 4)

And here is how my journal header function look like (To get org-journal to automatically set whether it is daily or weekly journal, check on Managing My History with org-journal):

(defun rz/org-journal-file-header-func (time)
  "Custom function to create journal header."
  (concat
    (pcase org-journal-file-type
      (`daily (concat "#+TITLE: Daily Journal"
                      "\n#+ALIVE: " (rz/calculate-week-counter 1999 1 4)
                      "\n#+STARTUP: content"
                      "\n#+FILETAG: :daily-journal:journal:"))
      (`weekly (concat "#+TITLE: Weekly Journal " (format-time-string "(%V-%Y)")
                       "\n#+ALIVE: " (rz/calculate-week-counter 1999 1 4)
                       "\n#+STARTUP: content"
                       "\n#+FILETAG: :weekly-journal:journal:"))
      (`monthly (format-time-string "#+TITLE: %m - Monthly Journal (%Y)
                                     \n#+STARTUP: content
                                     \n#+FILETAG: :monthly-journal:journal:"))
      (`yearly (format-time-string "#+TITLE: %Y - Yearly Journal
                                    \n#+STARTUP: content
                                    \n#+FILETAG: :yearly-journal:journal:")))))

(after! org-journal
 (setq org-journal-file-header #'rz/org-journal-file-header-func))

I personally find it quite interesting how weeks seems to sit right in the middle, It capable of helping me grasping the amount of time that I have; Day seems too minute, while Months and year seems to large. Yet week seems to be adequate.
